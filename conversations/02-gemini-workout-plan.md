Why PPL beats the "Bro Split" for Beginners
The main factor in building muscle and strength is Frequency.

Protein Synthesis Window: After you work out a muscle, it spends about 48–72 hours repairing and growing (this is called muscle protein synthesis). If you only hit Chest on Monday, your chest is done growing by Wednesday. It then sits "idle" for 5 more days until next Monday.

Volume over Time: In a PPL split, you hit every muscle twice a week. This means your muscles are in a "growth state" 6 days a week instead of just 1 or 2.

Compound Strength: Exercises like the Chest Press also work your shoulders and triceps. By grouping them (Push day), you allow them to recover together. In your suggested plan, if you do Chest Monday and Shoulders Tuesday, your shoulders are actually being worked two days in a row because they help with chest presses. Abs are muscles too: Just like your chest or biceps, your abdominal muscles need rest to grow.

The Best Approach: Treat your core like any other muscle. Train it 2–3 times a week with high intensity.
