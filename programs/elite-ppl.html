PUSH DAY A
Upper Chest Mid Chest Front Delts Side Delts Triceps Serratus
⏱ 65–80 MIN 🔥 STRENGTH FOCUS
COMPOUNDS FIRST
01
Flat Barbell Bench Press COMPOUND
Mid Chest · Anterior Delt · Triceps
King of chest mass. Full ROM, slight arch, retract scapula. This is your heaviest lift — push to near failure on final set.
4 × 4–6
⏱ 3–4 min
02
Incline Dumbbell Press COMPOUND
Upper Chest · Clavicular Head · Front Delt
30–45° incline. Stretch at bottom, squeeze at top. Upper chest is often underdeveloped — don't skip this.
3 × 8–10
⏱ 2 min
03
Seated Dumbbell Overhead Press COMPOUND
All 3 Delt Heads · Upper Traps · Triceps
Full shoulder mass builder. Neutral grip reduces shoulder impingement risk. Don't lock out — keep tension.
3 × 8–10
⏱ 2 min
ISOLATION WORK
04
Cable Crossover (Low to High) ISO
Lower & Mid Chest · Serratus Anterior
Constant tension throughout ROM. Hits the often-neglected lower chest. Squeeze hard at peak contraction.
3 × 12–15
⏱ 60 sec
05
Lateral Raises (Cable) ISO
Medial Deltoid
Cable keeps tension at the bottom of the movement unlike dumbbells. Lean slightly away. Do both arms, alternating. Key for shoulder width.
4 × 15–20
⏱ 60 sec
06
Overhead Cable Tricep Extension ISO
Long Head Tricep (biggest head)
Overhead position fully stretches the long head — the most growth-sensitive position. Rope attachment, full stretch each rep.
3 × 12–15
⏱ 60 sec
07
Tricep Pushdown (V-bar) ISO
Lateral & Medial Head Triceps
Follow extension with this for complete tri coverage. Elbows pinned to sides. Full lockout each rep.
3 × 12–15
⏱ 60 sec
CORE FINISHER
08
Ab Wheel Rollout
Deep Core · Rectus Abdominis · Serratus
Best deep core exercise. Start with kneeling version. Full extension, slow return. 3 sets is enough — quality over quantity.
3 × 8–12
⏱ 60 sec
⚡ PUSH DAY COACHING NOTES
  • Start heavy. Bench press when CNS is fresh. Never flip the order.
  • Mind-muscle connection on laterals — feel the delt, not your traps taking over.
  • Incline press is the most skipped exercise. It fills out the upper chest shelf massively.
  • Tricep long head (overhead extension) is 60% of your arm's size. Prioritize it.
PULL DAY A
Lats Upper Traps Mid Traps / Rhomboids Rear Delts Biceps Forearms
⏱ 65–80 MIN 💪 STRENGTH FOCUS
COMPOUNDS FIRST
01
Weighted Pull-Ups COMPOUND
Lats · Teres Major · Biceps · Rear Delt
Best width builder on the planet. Full hang at bottom, chin over bar at top. Add weight once you can do 10+ BW reps. Shoulder width grip.
4 × 6–8
⏱ 3 min
02
Barbell Bent-Over Row COMPOUND
Mid Back · Rhomboids · Lower Lats · Traps
Overhand grip, 45° torso angle, row to lower chest. This builds the thick, 3D back. Heaviest back movement — treat it like deadlift for the back.
4 × 6–8
⏱ 3 min
ISOLATION WORK
03
Single Arm Cable Row (High Elbow) ISO
Upper Lats · Rhomboids · Rear Delt
Elbow flared out hits the upper back. Elbow tucked hits lower lats. Use both variations across your training week.
3 × 10–12
⏱ 90 sec
04
Face Pulls (Cable Rope) ISO
Rear Delts · External Rotators · Mid Traps
Most underrated exercise for shoulder health and rear delt development. Pull rope to face, hands to ears. High reps, feel the squeeze.
4 × 15–20
⏱ 60 sec
05
Barbell Shrug ISO
Upper Traps · Levator Scapulae
Hold at top for 1 second, full drop at bottom. Don't roll — straight up and down. Heavy weight, controlled. Essential for thick neck-to-shoulder tie-in.
3 × 10–15
⏱ 90 sec
06
Incline Dumbbell Curl ISO
Bicep Long Head · Brachialis
Incline position fully stretches the long head at the bottom — peak stretch position triggers more hypertrophy. Don't swing. Pure negative each rep.
3 × 10–12
⏱ 60 sec
07
Hammer Curl ISO
Brachialis · Brachioradialis · Forearm
Neutral grip hits brachialis which pushes the bicep up, creating more peak. Also builds forearm thickness. Alternate arms each rep.
3 × 10–12
⏱ 60 sec
GRIP & FOREARM FINISHER
08
Dead Hangs / Farmer's Carry
Grip Strength · Forearm Flexors · Lat Activation
Hang until failure (3 sets) OR walk with heavy dumbbells for 40m x3. Grip strength directly improves every pull movement. Never skip this.
3 × Failure
⏱ 90 sec
09
Wrist Curls + Reverse Wrist Curls
Wrist Flexors · Wrist Extensors · Full Forearm
Superset both. Often neglected but builds serious forearm development and prevents tendon injuries. Light weight, high reps, full ROM.
2 × 20–25
⏱ 45 sec
⚡ PULL DAY COACHING NOTES
  • Pull-ups first, always. They require full CNS engagement and grip — don't fatigue these with rows.
  • Face pulls are non-negotiable. They offset all the internal rotation from pressing and keep shoulders healthy long-term.
  • Incline curls are superior to standing curls for hypertrophy — the stretch position matters.
  • Grip training is the cheat code to a stronger back. Your lats outgrow your grip — fix that.
LEGS DAY A
Quads Hamstrings Glutes Calves Hip Flexors Lower Back Core
⏱ 70–85 MIN 🦵 QUAD DOMINANT
COMPOUNDS FIRST
01
Barbell Back Squat COMPOUND
Quads · Glutes · Hamstrings · Core · Spinal Erectors
King of all exercises. Full depth (below parallel). Braced core, neutral spine, knees tracking toes. This is your heavy day — go heavy and earn it.
4 × 5–6
⏱ 4 min
02
Romanian Deadlift (Barbell) COMPOUND
Hamstrings · Glutes · Spinal Erectors · Lower Back
Hinge at hips, not waist. Bar drags down shins/thighs. Feel the hamstring stretch at bottom. Huge posterior chain builder. Follow squats while legs are warm.
3 × 8–10
⏱ 2.5 min
ISOLATION WORK
03
Leg Press ISO
Quads · Glutes (foot placement dependent)
Low foot placement = more quad. High and wide = more glute. Use this to absolutely torch quads after squats. Full ROM, no locking out. Go heavy.
3 × 10–15
⏱ 2 min
04
Leg Curl (Machine, Lying) ISO
Hamstrings (all 3 heads)
Curl to peak, hold 1 sec, slow negative (3 sec down). The slow negative is where hamstring growth happens. Don't rush this movement.
3 × 10–12
⏱ 90 sec
05
Bulgarian Split Squat COMPOUND
Quads · Glutes · Hip Flexors · Stabilizers
Single-leg work fixes imbalances. Front foot forward = glute focus. Upright torso = quad focus. Painful but brutally effective. Use dumbbells.
3 × 8–10/leg
⏱ 2 min
06
Standing Calf Raise (Machine) ISO
Gastrocnemius (outer calf)
Full stretch at bottom (pause 1 sec), full contraction at top. Calves need high frequency and full ROM to grow. Don't do partial reps.
4 × 15–20
⏱ 60 sec
07
Seated Calf Raise ISO
Soleus (inner/deeper calf)
Seated position isolates the soleus because knee is bent. Different muscle from standing raises. Both heads need their own exercise for full development.
3 × 15–20
⏱ 60 sec
CORE FINISHER
08
Hanging Leg Raises
Lower Abs · Hip Flexors · Hip Stabilizers
Control the negative. Don't swing. Tuck pelvis at top for full ab contraction. If too hard, do hanging knee raises. Grip training bonus.
3 × 12–20
⏱ 60 sec
09
Cable Oblique Crunch
Obliques · Transverse Abdominis
Side crunch with high cable. Rotate and crunch. Obliques give the waist that V-taper look. Do both sides. Don't skip — they complete the core.
3 × 15/side
⏱ 45 sec
⚡ LEGS DAY COACHING NOTES
  • Squat is king — don't skip it because it's hard. That's exactly why it works.
  • RDL immediately after squats is perfect — legs are warm, hinge pattern complements squat.
  • Bulgarian split squat will expose imbalances. Work through them — don't skip the weaker leg.
  • Calves need 2 exercises — one for gastroc, one for soleus. They're two different muscles with different fiber arrangements.

RECOVERY DAY

Muscle is built during rest, not in the gym. Use this day for active recovery, mobility, and nutrition to maximize the adaptation from your 3-day block.

🧘 10 min hip flexor & thoracic spine mobility
🚶 20–30 min light walk (blood flow, recovery)
😴 Aim for 7–9 hours sleep — GH peaks here
🥩 Hit your protein target: 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight
💧 2.5–3L water minimum — you sweat more than you think
🪡 Foam roll quads, lats, calves (5 min each)

COMPOUNDS FIRST
01
Standing Barbell Overhead Press COMPOUND

Lateral Raises (Dumbbell, 3/7 method) ISO
Medial Deltoid
3 full reps + 7 partial reps (top half) = 1 set. Brutal medial delt pump. Side delts are the key to wide shoulders and that "3D" look.

Dips (Weighted if possible) COMPOUND

Cable Crunch (Kneeling)

⚡ PUSH B COACHING NOTES
  • OHP standing builds full-body rigidity — it's not just a shoulder exercise.
  • Decline press paired with Day 1's incline press = complete chest development from top to bottom.
  • Weighted dips are one of the best mass builders — don't treat them like a warm-up.
  • 3/7 method on laterals: brutal but maximally effective for side delts.

FULL RESET

You've completed a full PPL cycle. This rest day is the most important of the week. Your body needs full glycogen replenishment and CNS recovery before starting the next block.

🛁 Cold shower or contrast therapy for recovery
🧘 Full body mobility flow: 15–20 min
🍚 Eat slightly above maintenance — refeed day
😴 Prioritize 8–9 hours — anabolic window is open
📓 Log your lifts and plan your next week's increases
🎯 Visualize your goals — mental recovery matters too

PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD SYSTEM

🏋️ Double Progression

Work within a rep range (e.g. 8–12). When you hit the top of the range with good form, add weight next session. This is the core mechanism.

📈 Micro-Loading

Add 1.25–2.5kg per week to compound lifts. Don't jump 5kg — micro plates are one of the best investments you can make.

📝 Track Everything

Log every set, rep, and weight every session. You cannot manage what you don't measure. No tracking = no progress.

⏳ Deload Every 6–8 Weeks

Drop volume and intensity to 60% for one week. This isn't laziness — it's when supercompensation peaks and you come back stronger.

😴 Sleep = Gains

GH is released during deep sleep. 7–9 hours minimum. This is non-negotiable. Missing sleep destroys half your gains from training.

🥩 Protein Target

1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight daily. Spread across 3–5 meals. Leucine threshold: 2.5–3g per meal activates maximum MPS.
